About Me

Hello! I am Shravan Godse, a final year undergraduate student in the department of Mechanical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay.

I am passionate about research in Materials Science

At IIT Bombay, I have been a convener of Krittika, The Astronomy Club. I am actively involved in mentorship programs at IIT Bombay. In my third year, I was the academic mentor for 6 sophomores guiding them in academic and general matters.

Apart from work, I love to read non-fiction and self-help books, watch TV-series (big fan of 'The Office') and stargaze (if the skies are clear :P).

If you ever want to talk about quantum physics, materials or The Office, ping me!

Research

My research interests include novel materials discovey, nanoscale tranport, thermoelectrics, semiconductors.
I wish to harness the "plenty of room at the bottom" to make efficient nano-devies especially for clean energy applications

In December 2019, I did a research internship at Varroc Engineering Pvt. Ltd., Pune, where I researched efficient and fast charging strategies for lithium-ion batteries for electric vehicles.

In the summer of 2020, I did a project involving Density Functional Theory and Materials Simulation using Quantum Espresso under the guidance of Prof. Dipanshu Bansal . You can find the final report here

Since July 2020, I am a part of the Materials Research Lab at IITB working under the guidance of Prof. Ankit Jain.

I am working on finding lattice thermal conductivites using first principles ab-inito technqiues and machine learning driven novel materials discovery. Our work on type-I clathrates is expected to be published soon!
For a sneak peak, have a look at the arXiv preprint

I also completed a research internship at the TheoFEM Lab at TU Munich, under the guidance of Prof. David Egger

I worked on vibrational properties (dispersions, IR and Raman spectra) of hybrid lead halide perovskite using first principles methods. I used VASP, Phonopy and SpectroscoPy codes.
